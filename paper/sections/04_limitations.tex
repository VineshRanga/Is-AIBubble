\subsection{Public Market Proxies for Private Foundational Lab Valuations}

The most significant methodological limitation of this study is the reliance on
\textit{secondary market transaction prices} and \textit{journalistic valuation
estimates} for the three principal IPO candidates: SpaceX (\$1.75T--\$2.3T target),
Anthropic ($\$1$T), and OpenAI (\$850B--\$1.1T) \citep{bloomberg_spacex_2026,
wsj_anthropic_ipo, ft_openai_ipo}.

Private secondary market valuations are subject to:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Illiquidity premia:} Shares trading on Forge Global, Hiive, or
        CartaX command a 15--40\% premium to theoretical fundamental value due
        to scarcity, which collapses at IPO \citep{bessembinder2020}.
  \item \textbf{Selection bias:} Only investors with positive views on a private
        company transact in secondary markets; information asymmetry between
        sellers (insiders) and buyers (outsiders) is not observable.
  \item \textbf{Valuation vintage mismatch:} The most recent private rounds
        for OpenAI (\$40B at a \$340B pre-money in March 2025) and Anthropic
        (\$7.5B at \$61.5B pre-money in November 2024) are structurally
        different instruments (preferred with liquidation preferences) from
        public common equity.
\end{enumerate}

The $\$210$B float supply drain estimate should therefore be treated as an
order-of-magnitude scenario assumption, not a precisely calibrated figure.
A 50\% discount to stated valuations would reduce the estimated drain to
$\$105$B---still the largest synchronised IPO liquidity extraction in market history
but materially different from the base case.

\subsection{Flat FCF Baseline Assumption}

The Monetisation Runway calculation (Section~\ref{sec:intro}) assumes that aggregate
hyperscaler FCF remains flat at its Q1~2026 annualised level. This assumption is
violated if:

\begin{itemize}
  \item AI application revenue materialises faster than modelled, generating operating
        leverage that expands FCF margins (upside risk to our thesis).
  \item Hyperscalers accelerate capex in response to competitive pressure without
        corresponding revenue acceleration (downside risk, consistent with our thesis).
  \item A Capex pause occurs due to balance sheet constraints or regulatory intervention
        (non-linear discontinuity our model does not currently handle).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Correlation Matrix Stability Under Compute Derivative Introduction}

The ScenarioStressTester calibrates its correlation matrix on the 2022 drawdown
window (January--December 2022). If a functioning compute derivatives market develops
prior to the 2026 IPO events, the hedging activity of hyperscalers and their investors
would alter the correlation structure between AI equities, volatility products, and
fixed-income instruments in ways our historical correlation matrix cannot capture.

Specifically, delta-hedging activity by compute option market-makers would introduce
a mechanical correlation between NDX realised volatility and GPU spot prices that
does not exist in our historical data. This represents an entirely unmodelled risk factor.

\subsection{Future Work}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Real-time index weight data:} Replace interpolated concentration
        estimates with actual daily constituent weight data from Bloomberg/Refinitiv.
  \item \textbf{Credit spread propagation:} Extend the VaR/PFE framework to
        investment-grade and high-yield credit spreads, modelling the second-order
        contagion from equity drawdown to corporate bond market liquidity.
  \item \textbf{Compute capacity utilisation rate:} Incorporate CoreWeave spot pricing
        or equivalent as a real-time proxy for AI infrastructure monetisation velocity.
  \item \textbf{Multi-asset PFE:} Generalise the PFE calculation from equity-only
        to a cross-asset portfolio that includes TIPS, VIX, and sector ETF exposures.
  \item \textbf{Bayesian scenario updating:} Replace fixed scenario probabilities
        with a Bayesian framework that updates as IPO filing dates and preliminary
        prospectus valuations become available.
\end{enumerate}
